# Brickfund — Marketing Strategy for First Users

**Version:** 1.0 · 2026-07-21  
**Goal:** Acquire the first **10 businesses** and **50 investors** without taking unsecured real capital until P0 money-path fixes ship.  
**Positioning:** *The marketplace where main-street businesses meet investors who believe in neighborhoods.*

---

## 1. Strategic stance

| Phase | Name | What users can do | Goal |
|-------|------|-------------------|------|
| **0** | **Waitlist + trust** (now) | Browse demos, join waitlist, book founder calls | 200 emails, 15 discovery calls |
| **1** | **Concierge MVP** | 3–5 hand-picked businesses; deals assisted offline | 3 funded stories (even if soft-circled) |
| **2** | **Self-serve beta** | List + invest with escrow after P0 closed | 10 listings, 50 investors |
| **3** | **City launch** | Lisbon (or one home city) density play | Local brand + press |

**Do not market “invest now” with live Stripe until auth + escrow safeguards are green.** Market *access*, *community*, and *upcoming inventory*.

---

## 2. Ideal customer profiles (ICP)

### ICP-A — Business (supply)

- Owner-operated café, restaurant, gym, barbershop, bakery, retail  
- Revenue already exists (not pure idea-stage)  
- Need €15k–€150k for expansion, equipment, relocation, terrace, second unit  
- Geography v1: **Lisbon metro**, then Porto / Madrid / Barcelona  
- Pain: banks slow; family/friends exhausted; doesn’t want full equity VC process  

### ICP-B — Investor (demand)

- Local professional 28–55, angel-curious, €1k–€25k tickets  
- Diaspora PT/ES wanting hometown impact  
- Small family office / syndicate lead  
- Motivation: tangible assets, neighborhood pride, diversification beyond stocks  

### Non-ICP (avoid early)

- Pure crypto degen capital  
- Pre-revenue app startups  
- Anyone expecting public-market liquidity

## 3. Value propositions

**For businesses**

> Raise growth capital from people who eat at your tables — with clear deal types and professional process.

**For investors**

> Back real local businesses you can visit. Loans, equity, revenue share — labeled clearly, terms transparent.

**One-liner**

> Brickfund is AngelList for main street.

## 5. Channel plan

### 5.1 Highest ROI (do these first)

1. **Warm network (week 1–2)**  
   - Personal WhatsApp/Telegram to 30 owners + 30 potential angels  
   - Script: 4 lines + link to brick-fund.com + Calendly  
2. **Local partnerships**  
   - Coworks, restaurant associations, gym franchisors, chambers of commerce  
   - Offer “capital education workshop” free  
3. **Founder-led LinkedIn**  
   - 3 posts/week: deal type explainers, owner stories, “why banks fail SMEs”  
4. **Instagram / TikTok local**  
   - 30s: “This café needs €40k for a second location — here’s how revenue share works”  

### 5.2 Medium ROI

5. **Google Business / Maps SEO** — city + “business funding” content hub  
6. **PR** — one exclusive to a local business outlet (ECO, Observador, Dinheiro Vivo, or city blog)  
7. **University / alumni angels** — Nova, Católica, IE alumni groups  

### 5.3 Later

8. Paid Meta ads only after waitlist conversion > 15% and listing inventory ≥ 5  
9. Referral: € spoils or fee rebate for intros that list

## 9. Trust & compliance messaging (mandatory)

Until legal counsel signs off:

- Frame as **community financing platform / technology marketplace**, not “guaranteed returns”.  
- Always: capital at risk; past performance N/A; may be private securities depending on jurisdiction.  
- Prefer **accredited / sophisticated investor** language for equity & notes.  
- Loans/revenue share may need local lending/consumer rules — **get PT/ES counsel before public raise**.  
- Replace fake landing stats with “early access” or real numbers only.

Add footer links: Risk disclosure, Terms, Privacy, Contact.

## 14. Success definition (first users)

You have “started” when:

1. **10 businesses** submitted real raise applications  
2. **50 investors** completed profiles (even if invest later)  
3. **1 public case study** of a structured deal (conceived or closed)  
4. Waitlist **> 200** with open rate > 40% on founder emails
